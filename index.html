Tihai — the Dancer's Triple Landing

A Tihai is one of Kathak's signature moves — a short footwork or spin phrase repeated exactly 3 times in a row, timed so the very last stamp lands perfectly on Sam (beat 1). Audiences often clap the moment they feel it land. Try a phrase length below and see if it lines up.

Lay — Speeding Up the Chakkars

In Kathak, dancers move through three speeds called Lay — Vilambit (slow), Madhya (medium), Drut (fast). When a dancer doubles their Lay for a flurry of Chakkars (spins), the Tatkar pattern doesn't change — it just gets squeezed into more beats. See how each beat shifts.
